Margaret Hamilton

Her Code Got Humans on the Moon

Hamilton during her time as lead Apollo flight software designer.

Here's a time line of Margaret Hamilton's life:

  • 1936 - born in Paoli, Indiana
  • 1954 - graduated from Hancock High School
  • 1958 - earned a B.A. in mathematics with a minor in philosophy from Earlham College
  • 1960 - interim position at MIT to develop software for predicting weather on the LGP-30 and the PDP-1 computers
  • 1961 - worked on the SAGE Project at Lincoln Lab (Semi-Automatic Ground Environment Wheather prediction system)
  • 1963 - NASA - joined the Charles Stark Draper Laboratory at MIT - working on the Apollo space mission, Hamilton's team was responsible for developing in-flight software
  • 1936 - Apollo 11 - Resolved the guidance computer's bug and saved the landing three minutes before landing
  • 1986 - became the founder and CEO of Hamilton Technologies, Inc.
  • 2016 - awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by U.S. President Barack Obama for her work leading the development of on-board flight software for NASA's Apollo Moon missions
  • 2017 - received the "Computer History Museum Fellow Award"
